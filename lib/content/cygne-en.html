The Black Swan

Those rare, unpredictable, high-impact events that our models systematically ignore.

Nassim Nicholas Taleb theorized it in 2007. Since then, the world has never stopped providing examples. Learning to recognize them is learning to think the unthinkable.

Explainer · The Black Swan 1 / 8

Why this concept today?

The Overton Window is one of the most useful tools for understanding how societies change, not through sudden revolution, but through the progressive shift of what is thinkable. In 2026, this mechanism is more visible than ever.

30
years separate "unthinkable" from "public policy" for mass digital surveillance. That was yesterday.

Essay & Concept

Dystopia in the 1990s, today it is legal infrastructure in most democracies. Not because citizens radically changed their minds. Simply because the window of what is thinkable shifted by degrees, each step making the next one possible.

The mechanism is not conspiracy. It is structural. And understanding it is what equips us to recognize it when it operates, on any subject.
