Valter Longo directs the Longevity Institute at the University of Southern California. He is the author of The Longevity Diet and the world's most cited researcher on therapeutic fasting and longevity. He founded L-Nutra in 2009, donating 100% of his shares to charitable foundations. He answers Soara's questions here.

Fasting has existed as long as humanity itself. Religions have practiced it for millennia. What has science understood that religions had sensed without being able to explain?

In general if you think about old practices that people have been doing for millennia, they do as much good as they do damage, fasting included. In fact, if you look at the data, you could argue that "fasting" in general does more damage than good. For example, the popular 16 hours of daily fasting is associated with higher CVD and mortality especially if breakfast is skipped. Even meal skipping is associated with increased mortality. Science is changing everything because it is identifying the type, length and frequency of fasting that is beneficial and that which is not.

You are Italian, you grew up in a culture where eating together is a fundamental act. How do you reconcile fasting with that deeply human and social dimension of food?

I reconcile it because I support only 2 types of fasting: 12 hours of eating per day and 12 hours of fasting per night — this is probably the easiest and safest type of fasting and does not interfere or alter "eating together". And periodic fasting mimicking diets to be done for 5 days maybe 3-4 times a year. These FMDs are only done for 15-20 days a year and also allow regular consumption of meals, only they are low in calories, plant based etc.

Bryan Johnson spends millions of dollars a year trying not to age. Silicon Valley billionaires are funding immortality programs. Does this movement inspire you or worry you?

I think is good to want to live long and healthy, let's say until 120. Living forever however, is a very different concept and it is much harder than people realize.

If your research succeeds and we live in good health until 120, what does that change politically? Who governs, who yields power, who decides when to step aside?

It changes very little. The population in most countries is already declining so if many made it to 120 it would not change much, especially if they got there healthy. Notably, only a small percentage is likely to reach 120 in the next 50 years, so we have to be much more worried about AI.

Conventional medicine took decades to take you seriously. What is it still refusing to look at honestly today?

Conventional medicine is conventional and does not want to change. It is actually generally a good idea to be skeptical of new things. However, there is a right way and wrong way to be careful. The right way is to always think about the patient and the complexity and making him/her healthy whereas the wrong way is to think about profits and have only a superficial view of the science complexity. This is why I introduced the 5 pillar method to make health decisions, in which clinical, epidemiological, basic research and centenarian studies are analyzed to reach conclusions.

Fasting is free. It is one of the only medical interventions that no one can patent and from which no one can profit. Is that why it took so long to be recognized?

Again, "the free fasting" does probably more damage than good in general, also because it allows people to avoid seeing a healthcare professional. It is not about being free or not, it is about being effective, safe and affordable by everyone. FMDs have a cost, but is not much more than the cost of food, and soon I hope governments will reimburse it. It has to be standardized and respectful of the medical process, but it needs to be smarter than drugs that block or activate a particular target like GLP-1 RA or statins.

You have worked with the centenarians of Calabria and the blue zones of the world. What have these communities understood about life that the rest of the world has forgotten?

Not much since now they have some of the worst health in Europe. Calabria and Campania now rank among the top for children overweigh/obesity in Europe. We did not learn from our own parents and grandparents who, because of a combination of factors, yielded many small villages with record numbers of centenarians, especially males. Those adherent to the mediterranean diet in Italy now are less than 20% and if we think about high adherence probably less than 10%.

Living longer also means living longer in a world that is changing faster and faster. Is longevity truly desirable if the planet we are going to inhabit for 30 extra years is degrading around us?

We can fix the degrading but we need to start immediately. It is very sad that we are fighting each other rather than working together to reverse this global degradation.

You say that cancer, diabetes and cardiovascular disease share a common cause: aging itself. If that is true, why does medicine continue to treat diseases one by one rather than treating aging directly?

Because having 10 drugs for each disease makes a lot of companies a lot of revenue. Part of that revenue is spent to influence doctors, journalists, televisions, shareholders, etc. If you turn on the television in the US, you see a drug commercial every few minutes. In Italy is not legal, but pharma companies have found a way to get around the law, for example by social media. They also keep finding ways to influence doctors. For example, by sponsoring clinical trials at the universities and medical centers.

In 20 years, will fasting be prescribed like a medicine and reimbursed by health systems — or will the medical establishment find a way to ignore it once more?

I think it will be prescribed and reimbursed but it will not be fasting it will be fasting mimicking diets or similar food-based interventions that have gone through the rigorous testing that drugs receive. Some with FDA approval some without it, but only after demonstrations in credible and large randomized clinical trials carried out by independent universities and medical centers.

What is the question I have not asked you that you wish I had — and can you answer it?

I have founded L-Nutra in 2009, a company that makes fasting mimicking diets. I do not take salary nor consulting from it and 100% of my shares have been and will be donated to charity and particularly the Create Cures and Valter Longo foundations in the US and Italy, committed to educating children and adults on "healthy longevity lifestyle" which is respectful of the environment but also dedicated to help patients implement integrative medicine solutions that combine standard of care with innovative and, in most cases, nutritional interventions. Without the company making the packaged FMD most of the trials would not have been done. Notably, the great majority of the trials were not sponsored by the company but by the government, foundation or the university. Many criticize me for doing this, but I believe I did the right thing.
